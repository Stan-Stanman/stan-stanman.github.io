S.Anna

S.Anna is a textual adventure game programmed in C that I developed from scratch with three peers as part of our 'Elements of Computer Science' exam, during the first year of our Bachelor's (04/2022 - 06/2022) at Napoli.

We, and especially me, are huge fans of point-and-click adventures, like the classic LucasArts games. Of course, since this was our first experience coding something from scratch, we decided to create an adventure game, but a purely textual one. The choice of C as the programming language was a requirement for the exam.

Personally, I contributed to the project by designing the exploration system. This system allows the player to move around a map, which is displayed as ASCII art, and to observe and pick up objects. To win the game, players must solve a puzzle to open the door to victory. Careful observation of the map and objects is crucial to finding the solution.

This experience was incredibly educational. It was my first real project developped with an engineering approach, and it was the moment I realized that a career in the engineering world was definitely the right path for me.
