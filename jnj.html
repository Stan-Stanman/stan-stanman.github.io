Project Overview

I was tasked with driving the early-stage development of a novel nail/screw system concept designed to address the complex biomechanical challenges of unstable femoral fractures. Taking the project from a foundational concept through to physical fatigue testing, I navigated multiple stages of the R&D lifecycle, bridging the gap between theoretical simulation and practical, tangible prototypes.

Key Phases & Contributions

Computational Modeling & Iterative Design

  • Focused on 3D modeling to obtain a CAD clinically functional and ready for a structural analysis.
  • Conducted rigorous finite element analysis (FEA) using Ansys to simulate standard fatigue testing setups. By analyzing these computational models, I proactively identified critical stress concentrations and iteratively optimized the implant's geometry to maximize fatigue life before committing to physical prototyping.

Design for Manufacturing (DFM) & Prototyping

  • Translated optimized 3D models into comprehensive, GD&T-compliant technical drawings.
  • Collaborated directly with the internal workshop machinists to ensure the designs were highly precise, manufacturable, and cost-effective, resulting in the successful production of functional prototypes.

Surgical Instrumentation & Systems Integration

  • Recognized that a new implant requires novel deployment tools. I collaborated cross-functionally to design and develop custom surgical instruments required for construct testing.
  • Developped the new instruments starting from the already existing instruments versions that I took from the industry PLM softwares, including Windchill and Agile e6.2.
  • Helped facilitate WetLab environments to test the ergonomic and practical application of these instruments in simulated surgical scenarios.

Empirical Validation & Fatigue Testing

  • Transitioned the project from the digital environment to the physical testing lab.
  • Currently managing the setup, calibration, and execution of dynamic physical fatigue tests to validate the Ansys simulations against real-world mechanical behavior.
  • Responsible for ongoing data acquisition, analysis, and compiling performance reports to inform the next phase of the product's development cycle.
